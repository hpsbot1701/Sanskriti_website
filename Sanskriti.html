About Us

The Sanskriti Committee of Meerut Institute of Engineering and Technology (MIET) is responsible for organizing cultural events and activities on campus. The committee is composed of student volunteers who are passionate about promoting and preserving the rich cultural heritage of India. Their primary objective is to provide a platform for students to showcase their talents in various forms of cultural expression, including singing, dancing, skits, nukkad natak, and other performing arts.

Our Objectives

The Sanskriti Committee has the following objectives:

  • Event Planning: To plan and organize cultural events and activities that are diverse, inclusive, and representative of the cultural heritage of India.
  • Talent Management: To identify, nurture, and promote the talents of students in various forms of cultural expression, including singing, dancing, skits, nukkad natak, and other performing arts.
  • Cultural Promotion: To promote and preserve the rich cultural heritage of India by showcasing it through various cultural events and activities on campus.
  • Event Management: To efficiently and effectively manage all aspects of cultural events and activities, including logistics, publicity, and promotion.
  • Publicity and Promotion: To effectively promote cultural events and activities on campus through various channels, including social media, posters, and word-of-mouth.

Events

The Sanskriti Committee organizes the following events throughout the academic year:

  • Introductory Event

    The Introductory Event marks the beginning of the cultural activities for the academic year. This event is designed to welcome new students to the MIET campus and introduce them to the various cultural activities that take place throughout the year. The event features performances by the Sanskriti Committee and other talented students, followed by an interactive session where students can get more information about the cultural activities on campus.

  • The Ad-Mad Show

    The Ad-Mad Show is a fun and exciting event that challenges students to create and perform a creative advertisement on a given product or service. The event is judged based on the creativity, originality, and effectiveness of the advertisement. It is a great opportunity for students to showcase their marketing and advertising skills.

  • Shayari Event

    The Shayari Event is a celebration of the art of Urdu poetry, where students can showcase their skills in reciting and composing shayaris. The event features performances by the Sanskriti Committee and other talented students, followed by an interactive session where students can learn more about the nuances of shayari.

  • Emir-n-Emira

    Emir-n-Emira is a cultural event that is organized in Meerut Institute of Engineering and Technology. It is a competition where both boys and girls showcase their talents by performing various tasks within a set time frame. The tasks can include activities such as singing, dancing, reciting poems, solving puzzles, and more. The winner of the event is usually chosen based on their overall performance, creativity, and ability to complete the tasks within the time limit.

  • Nukkad Natak

    Nukkad Natak is a form of street theatre that originated in India. In this event, Sanskriti Committee members performs a short play or skit in a public space, usually on a street corner or market, to convey a social message or raise awareness about a particular issue. The plays are typically performed in the local language and aim to engage with the audience and evoke a sense of social responsibility.

  • Shankhnaad

    Shankhnaad is a cultural event that celebrates the rich cultural heritage of India. In this event, MIET participants showcase their talents in various cultural activities such as dancing, singing, playing musical instruments, and skits. The event is typically organized in MIET college campus and provides a platform for students to express their creativity and showcase their love for Indian culture. The word "Shankhnaad" means the sound of the conch, which is traditionally used to announce the start of a cultural event in India.

The Selection Process for the Sanskriti Committee

  1. Resume Submission

    Second-year students prepare their resumes, highlighting their academic achievements, extracurricular activities, and any other relevant experiences that showcase their leadership and organizational skills.

  2. Personal Interview

    Selected students are invited to a personal interview with the Sanskriti Committee, which is conducted in a formal manner and is overseen by 4th year and 3rd year committee members. During the interview, the students are evaluated on their communication skills, problem-solving abilities, and their understanding of cultural diversity.

  3. Selection of Candidates

    After careful consideration, the committee selects 11 to 12 students, comprising six boys and six girls, to join the Sanskriti Committee. These students are chosen based on their overall performance in the interview, as well as their academic record and their involvement in extracurricular activities.

The Sanskriti Committee plays a crucial role in promoting cultural diversity and harmony at MIET College. As such, the selection process is designed to identify students who are passionate about preserving and promoting cultural values and who have a strong sense of responsibility towards their fellow students.

Overall, the selection process for the Sanskriti Committee is a rigorous and competitive process that aims to identify the most deserving candidates. The committee members responsible for conducting the interviews are experienced and skilled, ensuring that only the best candidates are selected for this important role.
